# 🎤 Speaker & Presentation Guide: Influish Tech Onboarding
This presentation guide is designed to help you smoothly walk through the interactive **Influish Tech Onboarding & Presentation** website. It provides core concepts, visual focal points, and punchy talking scripts for each section so you can keep your audience engaged and explain complex concepts effortlessly.

* **Target Audience:** Cross-functional teams (Product Development (PD), Business Development (BD), Marketing, Operations, and Executives).

## 3. 🍽️ Section 3: Tech 101 — Frontend vs. Backend
* **Visual Focus:** The split screen dining room vs. kitchen metaphor (with the animated pulsing connection dots passing back and forth).
* **Core Message:** Turn abstract tech jargon into a relatable story.

> ### 🎙️ Speaker Script:
> *"If you've ever wondered how developers talk about 'Frontend' and 'Backend,' think of Influish like a premium restaurant. 
> * **The Frontend is the Dining Room:** The tables, the beautiful decor, the friendly host, and the physical menu. It's everything you can see and interact with—like our mobile app screens and button clicks.
> * **The Backend is the Kitchen:** The stoves, the pantry, the chef preparing the meals, and the secret recipes. It’s our database and logic servers running behind the scenes.

## 4. 📲 Section 4: Mobile vs. Web (Key Differences)
* **Visual Focus:** The comparative list highlighting **Release Speed** and **User Adoption**.
* **Core Message:** Set expectations for why app updates take longer than website fixes.

> ### 🎙️ Speaker Script:
> *"One crucial point for our commercial and product teams is understanding Mobile vs. Web updates.
> * When we update our **Web Admin Panel**, it's **instant**. The moment the dev clicks deploy, every user sees it upon reload.

Plus, users have to download the updates manually. So, when planning campaign launches or quick hotfixes, we always buffer mobile store approval times."*

## 🙋‍♂️ Interactive Presentation Q&A Cheat Sheet
Here are the most common questions you might receive during the presentation and how to answer them with technical confidence:

### Q1: "Why do mobile app updates take longer than website fixes?"
* **Answer:** *“Web updates are uploaded directly to our servers, so everyone gets the new code immediately. Mobile apps are compiled packages that must be uploaded to Apple and Google stores, where they go through manual security reviews which can take up to 48 hours. Then, we have to wait for users to actually download the update.”*

### Q2: "Can the BD team ask developers to run a custom SQL data report directly?"
* **Answer:** *“To keep our production database safe and performant, all custom analytical reports should be coordinated through our Tech Lead so they can be scheduled and extracted safely during off-peak hours without slowing down the active mobile app.”*

### Q3: "How does the AI verify that an influencer actually posted the correct content?"
* **Answer:** *“The backend AI uses natural language processing (NLP) to read the post's captions and check for designated campaign hashtags, combined with computer vision to audit the images or video frames for specific brand assets or product placements.”*

## 🧠 Interactive Audience Checkups (Questions You Can Ask Them)
Use these quick scenario-based pop questions during your presentation to check your audience's alignment and keep the session active:

### ❓ Question 1: "Is this a Frontend or Backend Issue?" (Metaphor Check)
* **How to ask:** *"To check our understanding of the dining room vs. kitchen metaphor: If a creator taps the 'Submit KYC Documents' button, the screen displays a loading spinner and then successfully redirects them to their profile, but when Admin panel opens, the document files are completely blank. Where is the bug?"*
* **Target Audience:** Everyone (PD & BD).
* **The Answer:** *“The **Backend (Kitchen)**. The Frontend (Dining Room) did its job perfectly—it registered the click, showed the visual spinner, and moved the user to the next screen. However, the server failed to write the document files successfully into our database, or the Admin Panel is querying the wrong database path.”*

### ❓ Question 2: "Launch Day Planning" (Release Nuance Check)
* **How to ask:** *"We have a major brand campaign launching next Thursday morning. They need a custom submission review screen active on both the web admin and the creator's iOS and Android apps. When is the absolute latest our technical team should start uploading the final mobile builds for review?"*
* **Target Audience:** Product Development (PD) & Marketing.
* **The Answer:** *“By **Sunday night or Monday morning** at the latest. While we can update the web panel instantly on Thursday morning, mobile app updates require 24 to 48 hours for Apple and Google security audits, plus time for creators to download the updates.”*

### ❓ Question 3: "Staging Sandbox vs. Production Live" (Environment Check)
* **How to ask:** *"A prospective brand partner wants a live video demo of a new payment gateway flow this afternoon before they sign their contract. The developers just finished coding it. Which environment should we use to run the demo?"*
* **Target Audience:** Business Development (BD).
* **The Answer:** *“The **Staging Environment**. Staging is our replica sandbox where we can safely run test payments and walk through new code. We never show incomplete or un-audited builds in **Production**, as it could risk breaking real brand campaigns and live creator wallets.”*

### ❓ Question 4: "Handling Custom Data Requests" (Collaboration Check)
* **How to ask:** *"A brand wants a custom analytical report showing all lifestyle creators with an engagement rate over 4.5% by 5 PM. How should we coordinate this?"*
* **Target Audience:** Business Development (BD) & Operations.
* **The Answer:** *“Coordinate with the **Tech Lead** to schedule a SQL query extract during off-peak traffic hours. We should never ask individual developers to run ad-hoc queries directly on the live database in the middle of active system hours, as it can slow down the mobile app for everyone.”*

## 🎯 Alignment & Follow-Up Tasks for Your Teams
To ensure the PD and BD teams fully absorb and apply these onboarding concepts, suggest these **4 quick, practical tasks** at the end of your presentation. They are designed to build correct technical habits immediately:

### 🎯 Tasks for the Product Development (PD) Team

#### Task 1: "The Edge-Case Blueprint" (PRD Practice)
* **Goal:** Practice proactive scoping to prevent developer blockages.
* **The Task:** Ask PD members to take an upcoming feature on your roadmap (e.g., *"Allowing brands to pause a campaign midway"*) and write a 1-page mini-PRD that specifically maps out **3 clear edge-cases**:
  1. *What happens to influencers who have already submitted content but haven't been approved yet?*
  2. *How does the system handle wallets/payout funds that are currently locked in escrow?*
  3. *What visual state or banner is shown to active app users when they open a paused campaign?*

#### Task 2: "The Perfect Bug Report" (Jira & QA Practice)
* **Goal:** Master high-fidelity bug writing before launching UAT.
* **The Task:** Have PD log into the **Staging Environment** and write a mock Jira bug ticket for a simulated interface error. The ticket must strictly include:
  * Exact step-by-step instructions to reproduce the issue.
  * Expected behavior vs. actual behavior.
  * Phone device details (e.g., iPhone 15 Pro, iOS 17.4) and a mock screenshot attachment.

---

### 💼 Tasks for the Business Development (BD) Team

#### Task 1: "The 60-Second Tech Pitch" (Capabilities Practice)
* **Goal:** Translate technical strengths into immediate sales value.
* **The Task:** Ask BD members to select a real target brand in your active sales pipeline and draft **3 bullet points explaining why our tech makes us the best partner**:
  * *Example bullet:* "Because we use a Redis/BullMQ queuing system, you can run massive Instagram DM campaigns that send instant coupon codes to millions of users simultaneously during peak hours without our server slowing down or crashing."

#### Task 2: "The Clean Feedback Ticket" (Ops Practice)
* **Goal:** Cut down on developer back-and-forth queries when users report bugs.
* **The Task:** Have BD draft a simulated client issue report using our new **Structured Feedback Template**:
  * **Client Name / Campaign:** Brand X (Summer Skincare Launch).
  * **Visual Proof:** Linked screenshot of the specific error popup.
  * **Device Context:** Samsung Galaxy S23, Android 13, using the Chrome mobile browser.
  * **Timestamp:** May 22, 2026 at 4:10 PM.
  * **Exact Action Taken:** "Clicked 'Reconcile Payout' but received a 'Gateway 504 Timeout' error."
